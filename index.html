Physical Data Visualisation

How physicalization communicates and elicits reflections on data with the power of biology?

Bidesign | Edinburgh & New York | May, 2017

We focus on data visualisation in different way which can communicate and elicit reflection on data. The interaction using uboqiotous data looks like we are interacting with the physical world.

With the power of biology and IoT, we use bioluminescence bacteria as our media. IoT connects with users' devices and controls the level of oxygen depending on users' health data, then the installation can emit oxygen making bacteria light in different degree, which is a new way to express data in the physical world.

Twitter Shadow

Whether we can narrow the gap between social media network and physical word to encourage users to explore hidden stories behind social media data?

Digital Interaction | Edinburgh, UK | May, 2017

In this project, we focus on exploring the value of data behind the social media network. Through designing this digital installation, users can tweet anything about their comments towards buildings in Edinburgh online. Their comments can influence the popularity of buildings, providing potential options for people who want to explore this city.

Refugee Camps Button

How design changes the life and responses the need in deed.

Product Design | London, UK | Feb, 2017

It is currently hard to escape the news and ignore the major refugee crisis that is occurring in our world. We address the safety in refugee camps. It is a big concern, especially for women. Many reports show the high instances of sexual harassment, degrading body searches, and rape that female victims face. Our goal is to create a sense of community that values security and watching each other’s backs. Our second goal is to create a device that helps women rest with a peace of mind at night.

Although the origin intention is to create awareness within a vigilante community, we anticipate that it could be used in other positive ways. Buttons can be placed in potential "safe-zones" to alert other women. It is safe to sleep in a central area.

This project won the second prize in Cisco Challenge 2017 in UK.

Emotional Toaster

How design changes the relationships between people and products? What if products have emotions?

Emotional&Conceptual Design | Edinburgh, UK | Dec, 2016

From the point of view of philosophy, many people agree that objects have emotions. Paola Antonelli emphasised that the relationship between people and stuff had always been filled with powerful and unspoken sentiments, which can include attachment, love, even anger, etc.

I designed an emotional toaster to express product's emotional changes and users' attitude.
